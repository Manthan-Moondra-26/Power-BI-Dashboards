Starbucks Sales Dashboard (Power BI)
📌 Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze Starbucks sales data. The objective is to extract actionable business insights related to revenue trends, customer behavior, regional performance, and product distribution.

The dashboard supports data-driven decision-making for retail and food & beverage businesses.

📊 Key Metrics (KPIs)
Total Revenue: 1.49M
Total Orders: 100K
Total Stores: 500
Average Cart Size: 3.74 items
Average Customer Satisfaction: 3.69 / 5
🔍 Insights:
Revenue performance is strong overall.
Customer satisfaction is moderate, indicating scope for service or product improvement.
The average cart size (~3.7 items) suggests potential for upselling and bundling strategies.
📈 Sales Trend Analysis
🔹 Sales Trend by Month
Sales remain relatively stable throughout the year.
Noticeable peaks in March, July, and November.
Declines observed in February and October.
🔍 Insights:
There is a clear seasonal demand pattern.
Promotional campaigns should be targeted during low-performing months.
Peak months can be leveraged for premium pricing or new product launches.
🌍 Regional Performance
🔹 Revenue by Region
West region generates the highest revenue.
Northeast region shows the lowest performance.
🔍 Insights:
The West region represents a strong market and an opportunity for expansion.
The Northeast region may require targeted marketing, pricing adjustments, or operational improvements.
👥 Customer Behavior Analysis
🔹 Average Spend by Age Group
18–24 and 25–34 age groups show the highest spending.
Spending gradually decreases in older age groups, with 55+ contributing the least.
🔍 Insights:
Younger customers are the primary revenue drivers.
Marketing strategies should focus on youth-oriented campaigns.
There is an opportunity to increase engagement among older demographics.
🏪 Store Location Analysis
🔹 Revenue by Store Location
Suburban stores generate the highest revenue.
Urban and Rural stores perform slightly lower but remain comparable.
🔍 Insights:
Suburban areas may have higher purchasing power or repeat customers.
Expansion strategies should prioritize suburban regions.
Urban stores may require differentiation due to higher competition.
🥤 Product Category Analysis
🔹 Distribution of Drink Categories
Categories such as Coffee, Espresso, Tea, and Refreshers show nearly equal distribution.
🔍 Insights:
Demand is well-balanced across product categories.
No single category dominates, indicating a diversified product portfolio.
Opportunities exist to promote high-margin products selectively.
🍔 Food vs Non-Food Orders
Food Orders: ~68.42%
Non-Food Orders: ~31.58%
🔍 Insights:
A majority of orders include food items.
Food significantly contributes to overall revenue.
Bundling food with beverages can further increase average order value.
🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation
Data Modeling – Relationship building and structuring
Data Cleaning – Preprocessing for accurate insights
🚀 Conclusion

This dashboard highlights key business patterns:

Strong revenue performance with consistent sales trends
Clear regional and demographic differences
High contribution from food items and suburban stores

Overall, the analysis suggests opportunities in:

Targeted marketing (region + age group)
Upselling and bundling strategies
Improving customer satisfaction
